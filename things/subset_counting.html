Subset counting trick

Suppose we have two graphs \(H\) and \(G\), where we have that \(v(H) = k\) and \(v(G) = n\) (with a very large \(n\)).

Suppose further that every \(m\)-set of \(G\) has a copy of \(H\). How many copies of \(H\) do we have in total?

We may get a very easy lowerbound by double counting. Notice that every copy of \(H\) is in exactly \[\binom{n-k}{m-k}\] \(m\)-sets of \(G\).

As we have \(\binom{n}{m}\) \(m\)-sets, this means we must have at least:

\[\binom{n}{m} / \binom{n-k}{m-k}\]

copies of \(H\). ;)
